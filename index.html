Welcome to Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park a world heritage site is famous for the Great Indian One Horned Rhinoceros, the landscape of Kaziranga is of sheer forest, tall elephant grass, rugged reeds, marshes & shallow pools. Kaziranga National Park is a national park in the Golaghat, Sonitpur, Biswanath and Nagaon districts of the state of Assam, India. KNP has 5 ranges. The park, which hosts two-thirds of the world's Indian rhinoceroses, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

One Horn Rhinoceros

Embark on an exhilarating Jungle Safari in an open Jeep, where you’ll have the opportunity to search for the iconic one-horned rhinos, majestic tigers, elephants, jackals, langurs, monkeys, and various species of deer. Additionally, Kaziranga National Park is home to a wide array of domestic and migratory bird species, making every moment in the wild a memorable one. The park is divided into several safari zones, and you may need one or two days to thoroughly explore all that it has to offer. The jeep safaris are led by experienced wildlife professionals who guide tourists through these thrilling Jeep Safaris.

Kaziranga National Park is also known for being the residence of 'Big Five' animals, ie, Great Indian One horned Rhinoceros, Indian Elephant, Royal Bengal Tiger, Eastern Swamp Deer and Wild Water Buffalo. Kaziranga is one of the few wild breeding areas outside Africa for multiple species of large cats, such as Bengal tigers and Indian leopard.

Big Five Animals

Kaziranga National Park in Assam, India is home to over 480 species of birds, including migratory and resident species. The park is a great destination for birdwatching, especially from November to April.
Notable birds :
• Bengal florican: A critically endangered species and one of the park's most notable birds
•Slender-billed vulture: A critically endangered species
• Greater adjutant: An endangered species that migrates to the park from Central Asia during winter
•Pallas's fish eagle: An endangered species
• Greater spotted eagle: An endangered species
• Swamp grass-babbler: An endangered species
• Great Indian hornbill: A notable bird species in the park
• Indian roller: A notable bird species in the park
• Asian barred owlet: A notable bird species in the park
